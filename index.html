Personas

Understanding the Users

Created personas for the target market — working-class people, middle-aged market women, and university students — to ground design decisions in real user needs and behaviours.

Sarah Adeyemi

31 Years old · Lagos, Nigeria

Background

A market woman in Balogung, Lagos with a small business who needs a wallet to send and receive money, and manage profit savings by thrifting with other market women.

Goals

  • Create invoices to receive money from customers

Pain Points

Final Design

B2BSaaSWebFintech
ClientPurplegate Technologies
RoleProduct Designer

Problem & Solution

The Challenge

Problem

Small and medium-sized businesses often struggle to track income, expenses, sales, and staff activities efficiently. Manual processes make it difficult to generate invoices, forecast revenue, manage budgets, and detect fraudulent transactions — leading to poor financial visibility and decision-making.

Solution

The Business Finance Tracker provides a centralised platform for managing finances, sales, staff activities, and client transactions. It automates invoicing, supports budgeting and revenue forecasting, and delivers fraud alerts — helping businesses make smarter financial decisions and improve operational efficiency.

Design System

Typography & Colours

Inter was chosen for its clean, professional legibility across dashboard-heavy interfaces. A vibrant purple primary (#6A42C2) drives brand recognition, supported by an energetic orange (#FF7500) and teal (#1E9092) for data visualisation and success states.

Typography

Inter Semibold (48px) for headings, Medium (18–32px) for subheadings, Regular (12–16px) for body — consistent hierarchy across all dashboard views.

Colour Palette

Purple #6A42C2 (primary), Orange #FF7500 (accent), Teal #1E9092 (success), Amber #FFB44F (warning), Red #D62F4B (danger).

Dashboard

Core Screens

The main dashboard gives business owners an instant overview — total customers, income, expenses, P&L, predicted budget, income forecast, and fraud alerts — all in one glance. Financial metrics are visualised as a stacked bar chart across all 12 months.

Expense Tracking

Expense Management

The expense tracking view allows businesses to categorise spending (employee services, rent, utilities, marketing, travel), set budgets, and monitor predicted spend against actual — with full table-level filtering and category management.

Final Screens

Login, Mobile & Fraud Alert

The responsive design extends to mobile — login, expense tracking, and fraud alert views are all optimised for smaller screens, ensuring business owners can monitor their finances on the go with full feature parity.

Linqit is a cross-border fintech mobile app designed for Africans in the diaspora — enabling cheap, fast, and easy international money transfers, bill payments, currency conversion, and peer-to-peer swaps across multiple currencies and accounts.

B2CMobileFintechCross-border
ClientLinqit Finance
RoleProduct Designer
Year2022
PlatformsMobile · Web · Admin

Personas

Understanding the Users

Created a persona for the target market — working-class people, single and married middle-aged Africans in the diaspora who need to send money home and manage cross-border financial accounts.

Sarah Adeyemi

31 Years old · Newark, USA

Background

A Nigerian living in the USA with family and businesses in Nigeria that she sends money to often.

Goals

  • Send money to friends and family easily
  • Manage Nigeria and USA financial accounts
  • Pay for bills anywhere

Pain Points

  • Couldn't pay for bills from anywhere
  • Couldn't send money to other countries' accounts
  • Couldn't manage all foreign financial accounts

User Journey

Money Transfer Flow

The user journey for money transfer maps two distinct paths — Local Transfer and International Transfer — both starting from account selection and ending with a confirmed send, with different input fields tailored to each type.

Information Architecture

Structuring the App

The information architecture maps the full app structure from login — covering Home (accounts, transfer, bills, airtime, convert), Cards (view, create, options), Transactions, Peer-to-Peer (deals, swap), and More (settings, loans, customer service).

/ Keepevery

Case study · B2B · SaaS · Web · Business Management

Customer Communication
Platform.

Keepevery is a customer retention technology platform founded in 2021 that allows businesses to nurture long-lasting customer relationships through automated feedback collection, customer loyalty tracking, and rewarding CRM tools.

B2BSaaSWebBusiness Management
ClientKeepevery
RoleProduct Designer
Year2022
PlatformWeb

Overview

About the Project

Founded in 2021, Keepevery is a customer retention product that lets businesses manage and automate their strategy to keep every customer better. The solution includes customer feedback campaign management, customer health tracking, and a website builder — automating the customer feedback process end to end.

My Role

Responsible for requirement gathering from stakeholders and business teams, coordination of usability research, wireframes, mockup designs, design documentation, and prototypes. Worked closely with front-end engineers to ensure perfect interface translation.

Solution

Built a customer retention infrastructure that allows businesses to manage feedback campaigns, track customer health scores, and use a website builder — automating the entire customer feedback process.

Design Process

How I Approached It

A five-stage design process guided the project — from stakeholder discovery through to usability validation.

Step 1

Discover

Figured out what stakeholders wanted to achieve, conducted a heuristic assessment of the web app, and spoke with a few customers.

Step 2

Define

Researched the product in depth, conducted usability tests and user interviews, and identified key user pain points.

Step 3

Ideate

Generated concepts and drawings to figure out how to make various aspects of the product aesthetically pleasing and user-friendly.

Step 4

Design

Experimented with multiple approaches and devised solutions to make the app more user-friendly and efficient.

Step 5

Validate

Conducted a series of usability tests with users to validate the design and solutions.

Step 1 · Discover

Stakeholder Interview & Evaluation

Discussion with the stakeholder provided insight into what was required. Their main concern was the product meeting industry standards in ease of use and UI aesthetic, and they were searching for a means to automate the customer retention strategy experience. A heuristic evaluation identified two key gaps: users didn't have a seamless way to use feedback to inspire trust, and creating simple branded surveys was difficult.

Step 2 · Define & Analysis

User Persona

The user persona was created from user interviews — targeting business owners who need to manage and gather customer feedback at scale.

John Toye

Age 28 · CEO, Nadesk

Goal

Manage and gather a clutter of customers' feedback efficiently across multiple channels.

Pain Points

  • Tedious process of keeping up with customer feedback manually
  • Separating types of customers for rewards and reviews
  • Distribution of campaigns to multiple mediums

Step 3 · Ideate

Key Features & Wireframes

Key features identified to 10x the user experience: Feedback Management, Feedback Automation, Distribution Channels, and Website Builder. Wireframes mapped out feedback management details and the automated campaign creation flow.

Step 4 · Design

UI Design

The final UI covers the full product — home dashboard, feedback campaign management, create campaign builder, website template selector, website editor, customer management, team management, and campaign distribution setup.

Step 5 · Validate

Usability Testing

After the visual design was completed, the prototype was tested with 5 representative users. Participants were given two key tasks to perform: create a campaign and set up a website.

💬 "The participants were able to navigate through the app easily. They appreciated the new design and gave a few inputs."

RoleUX Research · UI Design

Overview

Goals, Role & Approach

My goal was to design a web and mobile app that allows students to develop their career, while giving recruiters an efficient way to discover and hire skilled entry-level talent.

Role

UX Research, Information Architecture, user flow, wireframes, UI design.

Problems

  • Recruiters spend time searching for students with skills on LinkedIn
  • No database for post-graduates looking for jobs
  • Recruiters are biased on race and gender
  • No way to verify job details
  • Job fairs cost a lot to organise

Solutions

  • Application automation
  • Easy job tracking for students
  • Easy onboarding for students
  • Job templates for recruiters

User Journey

No Long Wait Time

Two parallel journeys were mapped — students applying for jobs, and recruiters hiring talent — both designed to remove friction at every step, from sign-up through to application status updates.

Users Persona

Understanding the Student

Jack Raymond

Student · Canada

Background

A final-year student at Ontario Tech University looking for a way to get a paid internship after school.

Pain Points

  • Lack of transparency from recruiters
  • Tracking job applications is stressful
  • Companies sell student data to third parties
  • No paid internship opportunities

Goals

He wants to be able to get an entry-level job after school.

Wireframes · Student Side

Job Discovery & Profile

Design System

Components

Final Design

Job Listings, Profile & Interview Scheduling

Final Design · Recruiter Side

Posting Jobs & Managing Applicants
